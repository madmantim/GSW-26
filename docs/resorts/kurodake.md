## Terrain & Access Rules

### Off-Piste Policy
‚úÖ **FULLY ALLOWED** ‚Äî This is THE place for it!  
- **No rope enforcement** ‚Äî patrollers won't chase you
- Off-piste and backcountry are the entire point of Kurodake
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **NO AVALANCHE CONTROL** ‚Äî you're 100% on your own
- Must have proper backcountry gear (beacon, shovel, probe)
- Highly recommended: **hire a guide** (terrain is cliff-riddled and complex)

### Backcountry Access
- **Summit access via skinning** (1,520m chairlift top ‚Üí 1,984m summit)
- Huge backcountry area beyond summit (Daisetsuzan interior)
- 1-2 day traverse to Asahidake possible for advanced parties
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **NOT FOR US** ‚Äî need touring gear, ava knowledge, serious experience

### Terrain Zones

**Zone 1: Under the Ropeway (670m-1,300m)**
- Cliff-riddled, very steep tree skiing
- Technical chutes and couloirs
- HARD routefinding ‚Äî cliffs funnel you into specific lines
- Gets tracked quickly despite low crowds (limited options)
- Some shelter from wind/sun (holds snow quality better)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **GUIDE HIGHLY RECOMMENDED** ‚Äî wrong turn = cliff/death or brutal hike

**Zone 2: Under the Chairlift (1,300m-1,520m)**
- 2 groomed runs (beginner-friendly)
- Mellow off-piste
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **VERY FLAT** ‚Äî not ideal for snowboards
- Good views but not worth the trip alone

**Zone 3: Summit Skin Terrain (1,520m-1,984m)**
- Moderately pitched sub-alpine terrain
- Well-spaced trees, fun angle for powder
- More exposed to wind (can get slabby)
- Epic views on bluebird days
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Backcountry skills required** ‚Äî no patrol, no control

## Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

### The Reality Check
Kurodake is **not like other Hokkaido resorts**. This is:
- The steepest terrain in Hokkaido
- Unpredictable and often closed
- Requires advanced skills and route-finding
- Best visited with a guide unless you're very experienced

### Powder Strategy
- **Morning weather check is CRITICAL** ‚Äî ropeway often closed for wind
- If it's running, GO EARLY ‚Äî tracks fill in fast (despite low crowds)
- Focus on **ropeway terrain** for steep trees and technical lines
- Bring helmet, beacon, shovel, probe ‚Äî non-negotiable
- **GPS/topo map app recommended** ‚Äî easy to get lost/cliffed out

### Best Terrain (with guide)
- Tight, steep trees under ropeway ‚Äî some of the best in Hokkaido (Pete from Powder Project agrees)
- Technical chutes and couloirs
- Every line feels different (intricate terrain)
- Range from "fun-hard" to "hard-hard"

### Snowboarder Notes
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Chairlift terrain is FLAT** ‚Äî not ideal for boards
- Ropeway terrain is steep enough but very tight trees (need strong technique)
- If conditions are heavy/crusty, you'll struggle to turn in time to avoid trees
- If you're not confident in tight, steep trees, skip this resort

## Gotchas & Quirks

### ‚ö†Ô∏è WEATHER CLOSURES (BIG DEAL)
**THIS IS THE #1 ISSUE:**
- Ropeway closes frequently for wind, snow, avalanche risk
- Sounkyo is "the roof of Hokkaido" ‚Äî exposed, socked in, brutal weather
- Even when open, visibility can be zero
- **Maintenance closures:** Jan-Feb for several weeks (check dates!)
  - 2026 dates: March 2-19 (maintenance)
  - We're visiting Feb 6 ‚Äî should be open BUT weather-dependent
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **CHECK STATUS MORNING-OF** ‚Äî don't assume it's running
  - Call: 01658-5-3031
  - Website: [rinyu.co.jp/kurodake](https://www.rinyu.co.jp/kurodake/)

### üßó NOT FOR BEGINNERS/INTERMEDIATES

## Group-Specific Notes

### Is This Trip Worth It?

**PROS:**
- If conditions align: some of the best steep tree skiing in Hokkaido
- Almost zero crowds (empty car park = powder heaven)
- Off-piste paradise (no rope enforcement)
- Unique Hokkaido experience (this is NOT typical Japanese skiing)
- Sounkyo Gorge scenery is spectacular

**CONS:**
- High risk of closure (wind, weather, ava conditions)
- Requires advanced skills (not suitable for whole group)
- Needs guide for safety + finding best lines
- 90min drive from Asahikawa (long day trip)
- Limited terrain without skinning

### Decision Framework

**GO if:**
- ‚úÖ Weather is good (check morning-of)
- ‚úÖ Ropeway is confirmed running
- ‚úÖ Fresh snow in last 24-48hrs (makes the drive worth it)
- ‚úÖ We can hire a guide (safety + line knowledge)
- ‚úÖ Group is comfortable with expert terrain

**SKIP if:**
- ‚ùå Ropeway closed or forecast is bad
- ‚ùå No fresh snow (tracked steep trees = not fun)
- ‚ùå Group wants mellow laps (go to Kamui or Pippu instead)
- ‚ùå Can't secure a guide (safety concern)

### Backup Plan Options (Feb 6)
If Kurodake doesn't pan out:

- **Kamui Ski Links** (40min from Asahikawa) ‚Äî underrated gem, good tree zones
- **Asahidake (return visit)** ‚Äî ropeway may be more reliable than Kurodake
- **Otokoyama Sake Brewery day** ‚Äî tour, tastings, Asahikawa ramen lunch
- **Sounkyo Ice Festival + onsen** ‚Äî still worth the drive for culture/relaxation

### Snowboarder Considerations
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Chairlift terrain is FLAT (not worth it for boards)
- Ropeway terrain is steep but VERY technical (tight trees)
- Only attempt if:
  - You're very strong in steep, tight trees
  - Snow is light and dry (heavy = hard to turn in time)
  - You're okay with high consequence terrain
- If in doubt, sit this one out and go to Kamui instead
