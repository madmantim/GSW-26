## Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

### Powder Strategy

**1. Hit early AM** â€” freshies disappear fast near ropeway top

**2. Fan out wider** â€” fresh tracks available at lunch if you're willing to hike a bit

**3. Short hikes = big rewards** â€” sidecountry has steeper pitches, less tracked

**4. Alpine touring** â€” endless terrain above ropeway (skin/snowshoe required)

### Best Zones (Ropeway Accessed)

**TOP SECTION (Alpine & Upper Trees)**
- Reasonably open with short steep pitches
- Some natural hits (rocks, small cliffs, mushrooms)
- Gets tracked quickly but refills often with ~14m/year snowfall
- Skier's left has nice steep bowls (short hike required)

**MID SECTION (Trees)**
- Well-spaced pines and evergreens
- Pick your line â€” all routes lead down
- **Best powder:** Protected from wind, stays light and fluffy
- Beautiful tree skiing for advanced riders

**LOWER SECTION**
- Terrain flattens out
- Creek crossing forces exit via groomed trails
- Groundhog Day feeling (same exit runs repeatedly)

### Sidecountry (Short Hikes)
- Hike up/out from ropeway top for steeper pitches
- **Skier's left:** Short hikes access nice bowls
- **Skier's right:** Various lines but many flatten out or end at river (dead ends)
- Route-finding essential â€” easy to end up in closed valleys requiring trudging

### Alpine Terrain (Skin/Snowshoe)
- **Massive expanse** above ropeway
- Steep bowls, expert lines
- **Weather dependent:** Visibility often poor, snow can be wind-scoured
- **Hazards:** Fumaroles (stinky volcanic vents), cracks, poor visibility
- **Recommended:** Hire a guide for alpine touring
