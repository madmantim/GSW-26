    KAMUI SKI LINKS

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Small-medium (25 runs, 6 lifts: 1 gondola + 5 doubles)
    • Annual snowfall: ~8m (Central Hokkaido quality)
    • Vertical: 600m (150m - 751m)
    • Top elevation: 751m
    • Best for: Powder hunting, tree skiing, uncrowded off-piste, budget skiing
    • Season: December 13 - March 31
    • Trail Map: View Map
    • Operating hours: 9:00am - 4:00pm daily

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa (Downtown Asahikawa, 2min walk from station)
    Distance: 20.7km
    Travel time: 29 minutes
    Route: Via Route 12 (National Highway 12) west from Asahikawa
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at resort base - 2,000 spaces available - Easy access to base lodge and gondola

    Route notes: - Shortest drive of the trip (29min!) - National highway, well-maintained in winter - Straightforward route, no complicated turns

    - No pass-pulling risk - 4 named ungroomed tree zones (official resort areas): - Shinsetsu (fresh snow zone) - Tree Run A - Tree Run B - Tree Run C - Resort encourages tree skiing within controlled areas - These zones are ungroomed and subject to closure in poor weather/snow conditions

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Why Kamui is Special

    "The all-you-can-eat powder buffet" ‚Äî Kamui means "god" or "divine being" in Ainu mythology, and this place lives up to the name!

    Best Tree Zones (Snowboarder-Friendly)

    • Official Tree Runs (A, B, C)

      • Well-spaced pines and evergreens
      • Good flow for boards
      • Less tracked than famous resorts
      • Named zones feed back to lifts

    • Shinsetsu Zone

      • Ungroomed fresh snow area
      • Top elevation powder stashes
      • Check conditions daily (can close in bad weather)

    Powder Strategy

    • ‚úÖ HUGE ADVANTAGE: Uncrowded compared to Niseko/Kiroro
    • ‚úÖ Fresh tracks last MUCH longer here
    • ‚úÖ Weekdays are borderline empty (away from groomers)
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Weekends = school groups on groomed runs (but trees stay quiet)
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Gondola can have queues on weekends
    • Pro tip: Hit trees early, but don't stress ‚Äî this isn't a race like at Kiroro

    Snow Quality

    • Less snowfall than coastal resorts (8m vs 15-21m)
    • BUT: Often BETTER quality due to colder temps and Central Hokkaido location
    • "Up there as some of the best in the world" ‚Äî Powderhounds review
    • Excellent blue sky days (Hokkaido sunshine!)

    Navigation Notes

    • Easy to get lost in sidecountry (lots of traversing to return)
    • Snowboarders beware: Some sidecountry requires flat traverses
    • Consider hiring Kamui International School guide for local beta
