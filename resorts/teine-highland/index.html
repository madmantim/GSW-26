    TEINE HIGHLAND

    Base: Hotel WBF Sapporo Chuo (Central Sapporo)

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Small-medium (15 runs, 10 lifts total ‚Äî Highland Zone has 4 lifts)
    • Suitable for: Full day for powder hounds, half day for cruisers
    • Annual snowfall: ~10m+ (similar to neighboring resorts)
    • Vertical: 683m
    • Top elevation: 1,023m (Mt. Teine summit)
    • Best for: Steep in-bounds terrain, sidecountry/backcountry access, tree skiing
    • Season: Nov 22, 2025 - May 6, 2026 (Highland Zone); weekends/holidays only after April 6
    • Trail Map: Download PDF

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Hotel WBF Sapporo Chuo (Central Sapporo)
    Distance: ~20km
    Travel time: 40-45 minutes
    Route: Central Sapporo ‚Üí Route 5 (northwest) ‚Üí Teine IC ‚Üí Local roads to Highland
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at both Highland and Olympia base areas - Highland Zone parking is closer to expert terrain - Well-maintained access roads (standard winter prep)

    Route notes: - Shortest drive of entire trip (perfect for first day!) - Highway driving until Teine IC exit - Highland Zone is upper mountain (go here, not Olympia) - Can access via train + bus (JR Sapporo ‚Üí Teine Station + JR bus, covered by JR Pass)

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ PERMITTED IN DESIGNATED ZONES
    - Nature Zone (skier's right of Highland quad) ‚Äî marked off-piste area with ropes - Wide tree spacing, relatively mellow pitch - Gets tracked quickly (but less than big resorts) - Four sets of ropes mark the boundary (they really want you to notice!) - Standard disclaimers apply: ride at own risk, rescue at own expense - Patrollers are "incredibly lenient" (per Powderhounds) ‚Äî not pulling passes

    Backcountry/Sidecountry Gates

    üö™ TEINE GATE ‚Äî Legendary backcountry access near summit
    - Gate located near Summit Express top terminal - No registration required (ride at own risk stance) - Multiple bowls and chutes accessible - Can ride down to parking area (long run!) - ‚ö†Ô∏è Requires ava gear, experience, and local knowledge - ‚ö†Ô∏è Not for us ‚Äî we don't have touring/ava equipment

    Other sidecountry zones: - Under old cable car line ‚Äî "rite of passage for expert riders" - First to get tracked - Visible from resort - Skier's left of resort boundary ‚Äî multiple bowls, some wide chutes - Traversing + side-stepping required - Pink ribbons mark return points - Nasty cliffs if you go wrong (huck zone for pros only!) - Skier's right beyond Nature Zone ‚Äî exits to road near gondola - Short hike required - Less tracked than other zones

    In-Bounds Trees

    • Nature Zone ‚Äî best in-bounds tree skiing
    • Beautifully spaced trees (not too tight, not too wide)
    • North facing (powder stays pristine)
    • Consistent fall line
    • Short vertical (legs won't pump out)

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Best In-Bounds Tree Zones (Snowboarder-Friendly)

    • Nature Zone (permitted off-piste, skier's right)

      • Perfect tree spacing for boards
      • Wide enough to carve, tight enough to be fun
      • North-facing aspect = cold, dry snow
      • Feeds back to Highland lifts
      • Gets tracked by lunch on powder days (but still good)

    • Trees under Kitakabe Course

      • Midway down the "north wall" black run
      • Widely spaced
      • Steep sections (matching the 36¬∞ groomed run)
      • Access via Summit Express

    • Olympic Slalom/GS Courses (when left ungroomed)

      • Historic runs from 1972 Olympics
      • Technical terrain, good pitch
      • Bumps develop quickly
      • Great for leg burners

    Powder Strategy

    • Arrive EARLY ‚Äî 20min from Sapporo = day-trippers arrive fast
    • Hit Nature Zone first ‚Äî obvious powder zone gets tracked by 11am
    • Sidecountry = advanced only ‚Äî requires traversing skills and terrain knowledge
    • Consider a guide ‚Äî local knowledge unlocks the best stashes (gates, pink ribbons, safe lines)
    • Summit views = bonus ‚Äî stunning panorama of Sapporo city + Ishikari Bay

    Terrain Notes

    • Highland Zone is the goods ‚Äî don't waste time in Olympia (that's beginner zone)
    • Kitakabe Course ‚Äî Japan's most iconic steep in-bounds run (36¬∞ max pitch)
    • Name literally means "north wall"
    • Develops moguls quickly
    • Half-groomed sometimes
    • Short vertical ‚Äî 683m means quick laps, but legs don't get torched
    • Gondola = critical ‚Äî connects Olympia ‚Üî Highland (last one at 3:45pm/4pm)
    • Miss it = STUCK (no way back to Highland parking!)

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚ö†Ô∏è TWO ZONES = POTENTIAL CONFUSION

    Olympia = Beginner terrain (avoid unless picking up stragglers)

    • Zones connected by gondola + 6km flat connector run
    • Park at Highland if you want steep/powder
    • If anyone ends up in Olympia by mistake, gondola is the only realistic return

    üö° OLD LIFTS = SLOW LAPS

    • Paradise, Panorama No.1, No.2 = ancient double chairs
    • Summit Express (hooded quad) is fast ‚Äî stick to this
    • Old Olympic cable car = retired (was an "institution" but needed to go)

    üìè SHORT VERTICAL

    • 683m = quick runs (great for powder laps, but not a leg-burner resort)
    • Sidecountry can extend the descent significantly (if you traverse out)

    üå´Ô∏è FOG POTENTIAL

    • Coastal location = fog possible
    • Entire resort below treeline (visibility still OK in trees)

    üöå DAY-TRIPPER CENTRAL

    • 40min from Sapporo = busy on weekends/holidays
    • Weekday = quieter (Jan 29 = Thursday, should be good!)
    • Powder gets tracked faster than remote resorts

    üéø 1972 OLYMPICS NOSTALGIA

    Group-Specific Notes

    Snowboarders (Entire Crew!)

    • ‚úÖ Nature Zone = ideal tree spacing for boards
    • ‚úÖ Steep terrain = rare for Hokkaido (usually more mellow)
    • ‚úÖ No major flat sections in Highland (avoid Olympia entirely!)
    • ‚úÖ Gondola = hooded (board-friendly, no sketchy T-bars)
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Sidecountry traversing = may require unhooking (locals know best lines)

    First Day Strategy

    This is Day 1 of skiing ‚Äî be smart: - Don't blow legs out immediately (short vertical = manageable) - Warm up on Panorama runs, progress to Kitakabe steeps - Test gear, get ski legs back - Nature Zone trees = good confidence builder - Save energy for 16 more days of skiing!

    Timing Considerations

    • Dinner reservation: Tall Sun Dining Izakaya at 18:30 (see itinerary)
    • Onsen option: Teine Onsen Honoka (5min away) = perfect post-ski, pre-dinner
    • Drive back: 40-45min to hotel (need to leave by ~5:30pm for relaxed schedule)
    • Realistic ski day: 9am first lift ‚Üí 3:30pm last run = 6.5 hours (plenty!)

    Weather Considerations

    • Check forecast morning-of ‚Äî coastal weather can change
    • Fog backup plan: Stick to trees (Nature Zone, under Kitakabe)
    • Wind closures: Less common than higher elevation resorts (below treeline)
