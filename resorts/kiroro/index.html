    KIRORO RESORT

    Base: Hotel WBF Sapporo Chuo

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Medium (21 runs, 9 lifts)
    • Suitable for: 3-4 day stay maximum
    • Annual snowfall: ~21m (one of the highest in Hokkaido)
    • Vertical: 610m
    • Top elevation: 1,180m
    • Best for: Tree skiing, powder stashes, off-piste terrain
    • Trail Map: Download PDF

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Hotel WBF Sapporo Chuo (Central Sapporo)
    Distance: ~60km
    Travel time: ~90 minutes
    Route: Sapporo ‚Üí Route 5 (Otaru direction) ‚Üí Route 393 (Akaigawa)
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at resort base - Multiple lots near gondola station - Well-marked, easy access

    Route notes: - Mostly highway driving until Akaigawa turnoff - Well-maintained winter roads (standard for Hokkaido) - Nikka Yoichi Whisky Distillery is ~20min detour on route (see Nearby Attractions)

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED ‚Äî This is huge for us!
    - No pass-pulling risk (unlike old days) - 14 named off-piste zones including: - Powder Ridge - Deep Pine - Orange Light - Peaches - Leaping Rabbit - Monkey Park - Bear Trap - Flashman

    Sidecountry/Backcountry

    • Gates available ‚Äî requires submitting climbing plans at main lodge
    • Backcountry access permitted with proper registration
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Not for us (no touring gear/ava equipment planned)

    In-Bounds Trees

    • Well-marked forested off-piste areas
    • Ideal for our crew ‚Äî short hikes, feeds back to lifts
    • Snowboarder-friendly (well-spaced trees)

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Best Tree Zones (Snowboarder-Friendly)

    Based on powderhounds.com + off-piste international:

    • Deep Pine (off-piste zone #2)

      • Well-spaced pines and evergreens
      • Feeds back to main runs
      • Short hike from Asari lifts

    • Monkey Park (off-piste zone #6)

      • Classic Kiroro tree run
      • Medium density, good flow for boards
      • Accessed via Nagamine area

    • Powder Ridge (off-piste zone #1)

      • Top elevation powder zone
      • Best after fresh snow
      • Can get tracked fast (Club Med crowds)

    Powder Strategy

    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Fresh tracks disappear FAST (Club Med + growing popularity)
    • Hit trees early AM ‚Äî crowds stick to groomers first
    • Off-piste zones are marked but require navigation skills
    • Hire a guide? Recommended if we want deep local knowledge (trees/rivers can be tricky)

    Terrain Notes

    • On-piste: 1/3 beginner, 1/3 intermediate, 1/3 advanced (but not super challenging)
    • Real goods: Off-piste trees + ungroomed runs
    • Mogul courses available (locals love 'em)
    • Grand Line = best for cruising/warm-up laps

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Asari Peak area ‚Äî steeper terrain, better snow
    • Named off-piste zones (see above)
    • Ungroomed runs for powder (several blue/black runs left natural)

    SKIPPABLE

    • Family Course ‚Äî mellow beginner zone
    • Center Link ‚Äî connector run, nothing special

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • Grand Line ‚Äî long cruiser, good for tired legs
    • Yoichi Course 1-A ‚Äî mellow blue, scenic

      • Take-out available
      • Quick refuel option

    SNACKS & DRINKS

    • ¬•500 deposit for KIROCA (lift IC card) ‚Äî refundable

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚ö†Ô∏è WIND CLOSURES

    BIG ISSUE: Gondola & top lifts close with moderate wind (not just extreme)
    - Recent trend at Kiroro (last few years) - Check lift status morning-of: Kiroro Dashboard - Backup plan: Focus on lower lifts if windy

    üí∞ UPSCALE PRICING

    • Resort caters to high-end market (Club Med clientele)
    • Food, rentals, everything costs more than typical Japanese resort
    • Budget accordingly

    üè® NO VILLAGE

    • Resort complex only (Club Med + hotels)
    • Limited Japanese culture/local vibe
    • Nightlife = resort bars/restaurants only

    üå®Ô∏è FRESH TRACKS COMPETITION

    • Club Med + growing popularity = powder gets tracked fast
    • Need to be strategic (early AM, off-piste zones, trees)
    • Consider guide if we want primo stashes

    Nearby Attractions (On Travel Path)

    ü•É Nikka Yoichi Whisky Distillery

    Distance: ~20min detour from Sapporo-Kiroro route (via Otaru)
    Location: Yoichi (between Otaru and Kiroro)
    Why visit: One of Japan's legendary whisky makers, still uses traditional coal-fired distillation
    Tour: Guided tours available (reservation required)
    Access: 6min walk from Yoichi Station
    Time needed: 1-2 hours
    Website: Nikka Whisky Yoichi

    üç∫ Otaru Beer (Otaru Warehouse No.1)

    Distance: ~30min from Kiroro
    Why visit: Authentic German-style beers, traditional Reinheitsgebot brewing
    Tour: Free brewery tours every 30min (11:10am-5:40pm, no reservation needed)
    Time needed: 1 hour
    Location: Along the picturesque Otaru Canal

    Rating: 4.3‚òÖ on Google

    üìç Otaru City

    Distance: 28km from Kiroro (~30min)

    Good for: Late lunch/early dinner on way back to Sapporo
