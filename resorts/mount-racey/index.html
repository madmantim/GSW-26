    MOUNT RACEY (YUBARI RESORT)

    Base: Tomamu The Tower (checking out after skiing)

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Small (4-5 lifts, 18 trails)
    • Suitable for: Half-day visit, morning powder session
    • Annual snowfall: ~8m (315 inches average)
    • Vertical: 408m (1,326 ft)
    • Elevation: 294m base, 702m summit
    • Best for: Tree skiing, powder stashes, uncrowded runs, stormy-day backup
    • Trail Map: View Map

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Tomamu The Tower (leaving after skiing)
    Distance: ~85km
    Travel time: ~1.5 hours
    Route: Tomamu ‚Üí Route 38 ‚Üí Route 274 west ‚Üí Yubari
    Google Maps: View route

    After skiing: Drive to Asahikawa (~100km, ~1.5-2 hours)
    Full day route: Tomamu ‚Üí Mt Racey ‚Üí Asahikawa (~185km total, ~3-3.5 hours driving + skiing)

    Parking:
    - Free parking at resort base - Easy access near Shuttle Base building

    Strategic Note:
    This is a transition day ‚Äî ski the morning at Mt Racey, then drive to Asahikawa for our next phase. Pack cars before leaving Tomamu!

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED ‚Äî Tree skiing is actively encouraged!
    - Multiple "in-bounds" tree zones - Some sidecountry available (not officially marked) - No pass-pulling for skiing trees within resort boundary - Powderhounds says: "Either side of 'Thrilling Line' is where the real thrills are"

    Sidecountry/Backcountry

    • Some sidecountry accessible, but not well-documented
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Not for us (we're just here for a quick morning session)

    In-Bounds Trees

    • This is the highlight ‚Äî well-spaced trees, powder stashes
    • Often untouched due to low crowds (mostly school groups)
    • Sheltered and lower elevation = good for stormy days
    • Snowboarder-friendly tree spacing

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Best Tree Zones (Snowboarder-Friendly)

    Based on Powderhounds + local beta:

    • Either side of "Thrilling Line"

      • Official course is boring (7¬∞ average pitch), but the trees are gold
      • Well-spaced, powder holds well
      • Feeds back to main lifts

    • "In-bounds" tree zones throughout

      • Not heavily marked, but patrolled
      • Look for natural gaps in tree cover
      • Very few people ski these ‚Äî you'll likely have them to yourself

    • Off-piste stashes

      • Multiple unmarked powder zones
      • Low visitation = fresh tracks hours after snowfall

    Powder Strategy

    • üéø Arrive early ‚Äî we want first tracks before the school groups
    • üå®Ô∏è Low crowds ‚Äî powderhounds report "you'll have the powder all to yourself"
    • üå≤ Focus on trees ‚Äî on-piste is tame, trees are where the fun is
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Gondola closed ‚Äî Shuttle 6 gondola is NOT operating 2025-26 season (maintenance)
    • üí® Wind backup ‚Äî lower elevation + sheltered = good option when other resorts blow out

    Terrain Notes

    • On-piste: 20% beginner, 40% intermediate, 40% "advanced" (but not challenging)
    • Real terrain: Trees, off-piste, natural features
    • "Expert" runs: Marketing fluff ‚Äî one hits 45¬∞ for a second, most are easy
    • For us: Skip the groomers, hunt the trees

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Tree zones on both sides of Thrilling Line ‚Äî best powder stashes
    • Any in-bounds trees ‚Äî uncrowded, great snow
    • Early morning laps ‚Äî beat the school groups

    SKIPPABLE

    • Thrilling Line (official course) ‚Äî ironically not thrilling (7¬∞ average pitch)
    • Beginner zones ‚Äî we're here for powder, not learner slopes

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • Intermediate groomers ‚Äî easy cruisers if legs are tired from Tomamu
    • Lower elevation runs ‚Äî mellow, scenic

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚ö†Ô∏è GONDOLA CLOSED THIS SEASON

    BIG CHANGE: Shuttle 6 gondola (6-person) is NOT operating 2025-26 season due to maintenance
    - Sky Station (mountain top building) is also closed - Lifts operating: Beginners 4 (quad), Dancers 4 (quad), Swingers 2 (double) - Racers 2 lift is also closed - Impact: Reduced uphill capacity, but less terrain closed (trees still accessible)

    üíÄ DESTITUTE TOWN VIBES

    • Yubari was a coal-mining town of 120,000, now ~8,000 (mostly elderly)
    • Resort has had financial troubles, hotel at base is closed
    • "Ghost town" atmosphere in Yubari city
    • Why visit? You're supporting a struggling community + uncrowded powder!

    üè® NO ACCOMMODATION

    • Hotel Mount Racey (ski-in/out) is CLOSED
    • Hotel Shuparo also closed
    • Only option: Hostel Himawari (old school barracks)
    • For us: N/A ‚Äî we're staying at Tomamu and leaving after skiing

    üå¶Ô∏è WEATHER ADVANTAGE

    • Lower elevation = less wind holds than higher resorts
    • Sheltered location = good backup when Tomamu/Furano blow out
    • Stable weather throughout season (per resort marketing)

    üë• CROWDS

    • Mostly school groups (identically dressed kids)
    • Very few foreigners (you'll likely be the only ones)
    • Much less crowded than Niseko or other famous resorts
    • Powder stays fresh longer

    Time needed: 30-45 minutes
    Worth it? Only if you're into industrial history ‚Äî otherwise skip to get to Asahikawa

    üçà Yubari Melon

    What: Yubari is famous for insanely expensive melons (¬•2-3 million at auction!)
    In February: Not melon season (summer crop)
    Skip it: Not relevant for winter visit

    üó∫Ô∏è Route Options to Asahikawa

    Option 1: Direct Route (Recommended) - Yubari ‚Üí Route 274 ‚Üí Iwamizawa ‚Üí Route 12 ‚Üí Asahikawa - ~100km, ~1.5-2 hours - Fastest, well-maintained winter roads

    Option 2: Scenic Route via Furano/Biei - Yubari ‚Üí Furano ‚Üí Biei ‚Üí Asahikawa - ~130km, ~2.5 hours - Biei = stunning winter farmland scenery (patchwork hills, snow-covered fields) - Furano = ski town (we're skiing there Feb 4) - Worth the extra 30min if weather is clear and we have time

    ‚è∞ Timing Strategy

    Alternative: If fresh snow, ski until 3pm and push dinner back

    Snowboarders

    • ‚úÖ Tree spacing is excellent (low-density, natural flow)
    • ‚úÖ No flat sections in tree zones
    • ‚úÖ Lower elevation = softer snow (less chance of ice)
    • ‚úÖ Uncrowded = no speed checks

    Strategy for the Day

    Goals: 1. ‚úÖ Get a solid 3-4 hours of tree skiing in fresh pow 2. ‚úÖ Efficiently transition from Tomamu to Asahikawa 3. ‚úÖ Arrive in Asahikawa with time to settle before dinner

    What this is NOT: - ‚ùå Not a full-day resort (too small) - ‚ùå Not a tourist destination (destitute town) - ‚ùå Not a luxury experience (basic facilities)

    What this IS: - ‚úÖ Uncrowded powder stash - ‚úÖ Great tree skiing - ‚úÖ Logistically smart stop between Tomamu and Asahikawa - ‚úÖ Authentic "local Japan" experience

    Weather Consideration

    • Check Tomamu forecast the night before
    • If Tomamu has fresh snow + good visibility, consider extending Tomamu and skipping Mt Racey
    • If Tomamu is windy/poor visibility, Mt Racey is the smart play (lower elevation, sheltered)
    • Flexibility is key ‚Äî don't lock into Mt Racey if Tomamu is dumping

    - This may bring more international visitors in future years - Currently still very local/uncrowded - Good sign for resort's financial viability
