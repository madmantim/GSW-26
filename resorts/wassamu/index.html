    WASSAMU HIGASHIYAMA SKI AREA

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Tiny (7-8 courses, 2 lifts)
    • Vertical: 193-201m (154-347m elevation / 153-354m elevation)
    • Lifts: Pair lift (574m) + single chair (309m)
    • Annual snowfall: Not measured (moderate, typical for Northern Hokkaido)
    • Season: Early December to mid-March
    • Best for: Local gem vibes, untouched powder late in the day, low crowds
    • Trail Map: View Map
    • Max gradient: 30 degrees (on-piste)

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa
    Distance: ~41km north
    Travel time: ~45-50 minutes
    Route: Asahikawa ‚Üí Route 40 north (Soya Main Line) ‚Üí Wassamu
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at resort base - Along national highway, very easy access - 5 minutes from Wassamu Expressway exit

    Route notes: - Straight shot north on Route 40 (Soya Main Line) - Along JR Soya Main Line ‚Äî can see Wassamu Station from highway - Walking distance from Wassamu town center - Well-maintained winter road (main artery to Nayoro/Wakkanai) - Evening: Continue north on Route 40 to Nayoro Onsen Sunpillar (~45km, 40min)

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED IN DESIGNATED AREAS
    - Some ungroomed/unmarked zones in the trees (shown in red on course map) - "Sidecountry" zones accessible from lifts - ‚ö†Ô∏è Not all tree zones are official ‚Äî assess risk yourself - Resort has limited rescue resources (small town-run operation)

    Tree Zones

    Skier's Right (from top of single chair): - Trees of varying spacing - Moderate pitch at top, mellows toward base - Some small creeks, gullies, and a canal to navigate - Can bail early or ride to base - Snowboarder-friendly (not too tight)

    Skier's Left (from top): - Fun but less vertical - Shorter runs than right side

    Course Exterior: ‚õî CLOSED ‚Äî Forest outside marked courses is off-limits

    In-Bounds Terrain

    • 7-8 named courses (mix of groomed and ungroomed)
    • Courses marked in red on map = ungroomed (powder zones!)
    • Some courses groomed depending on conditions/competitions
    • Beginner trails: A couple from top lift along periphery (but not ideal for nervous beginners)
    • Intermediate: Most groomed piste
    • Advanced: Trees and ungroomed sections

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    THE WASSAMU SECRET ü§´

    This is the dream: Opens at 10am (leisurely!) and powder stays untouched until late in the day. Zero crowds. Zero competition. If you've been dealing with tracked-out resorts, this is your therapy day.

    Powder Strategy

    • No rush! ‚Äî Seriously. Sleep in. The powder will wait.
    • Hit the trees mid-morning ‚Äî Still untracked at 11am, noon, even 2pm
    • Lap the ungroomed zones (marked red on map) ‚Äî Locals might not even touch them
    • Single chair = goods ‚Äî Top lift accesses the best tree zones
    • Explore both sides ‚Äî Skier's right has more vertical, left is quicker laps

    Terrain Notes

    • Max gradient 30¬∞ ‚Äî Not scary steep, but pitchy enough to be fun
    • Trees are well-spaced ‚Äî Great for snowboards (no tight squeeze)
    • Small creeks/gullies ‚Äî Pay attention, especially in low snow
    • Canal near base ‚Äî Easy to spot and avoid
    • Cross-country tracks at base ‚Äî If anyone's feeling XC vibes

    What Makes This Special

    • Town-run operation ‚Äî No corporate vibe, pure local ski hill
    • Powder persistence ‚Äî Because it's so unknown, fresh tracks last ALL DAY
    • Sunny aspect + low elevation ‚Äî Perfect for cold days (which are common in Dohoku in Feb)
    • Training ground for Olympians ‚Äî Yuka Takeuchi (Sochi 2014 silver medalist) trained here!

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Single chair tree zones (both sides) ‚Äî The real reason you're here
    • Ungroomed red courses ‚Äî Powder playgrounds
    • Top-to-bottom runs (~1km) ‚Äî Short but sweet

    SKIPPABLE

    • Nothing really ‚Äî it's all good for a small hill
    • Beginner trails if you want pitch

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • Groomed courses from pair lift ‚Äî Mellow, wide, perfect for warm-up
    • Periphery beginner trails ‚Äî Easy cruise, scenic views

    On-Mountain Food

    LUNCH

    Renovated Lodge Restaurant (2022 remodel) - ‚≠ê Fresh interior, small-town quality - Noodles (ramen, udon) - Curry rice - Donburi (rice bowls) - Locals love it ‚Äî "The restaurant is delicious" (TripAdvisor review) - Bring your own food allowed (rare in Japan!) - Small raised tatami seating area (koagari)

    FACILITIES

    • Bright, airy lodge with vaulted ceilings
    • Drink vending machines
    • Clean, modern toilets (including multi-purpose with baby changing)
    • Ticket counter
    • Food truck sometimes in parking lot ‚Äî Yakitori (grilled chicken skewers)!

    VIBE

    • Zero frills, maximum charm
    • Families, local kids, ski club racers training
    • You might be the only tourists there

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚è∞ LATE OPENING (10AM)

    • Not a 9am resort ‚Äî embrace the slow morning
    • Check their Twitter/Facebook for daily status
    • Inconsistent lift operations ‚Äî Sometimes only 1 lift runs
    • Weather/staffing can affect which lifts operate

    üìÖ RACE SCHEDULE (2025-26)

    Feb 9 = CLEAR! No races scheduled on our day.

    ‚òÄÔ∏è SUNNY ASPECT

    • Southwest-facing slopes
    • Great when it's cold (which it will be in Northern Hokkaido in Feb)
    • Can get sun-affected if temps rise (rare in Feb)

    üå°Ô∏è LOW ELEVATION (154-347m)

    • Watch the forecast ‚Äî needs cold temps for good snow quality
    • If forecast is warm (above 0¬∞C), consider alternatives

    üèîÔ∏è SMALL TERRAIN

    • Only ~200m vertical ‚Äî you'll lap this quickly
    • Don't expect variety like Niseko or Furano
    • Think of it as a powder buffet, not a resort tour

    üöë LIMITED RESOURCES

    • No major rescue/patrol infrastructure
    • If you venture into sidecountry, you're on your own
    • Stay smart, stay visible, stay together

    Nearby Attractions (On Travel Path)

    Route Context

    We're traveling Asahikawa ‚Üí Wassamu ‚Üí Nayoro along Route 40 (the main north-south artery of Hokkaido). This is a rural, agricultural region ‚Äî expect farmland, small towns, and local character.

    üèîÔ∏è Horokanai Sanmen-yama (Backcountry Peak)

    Distance: ~20km north of Wassamu (toward Nayoro)
    Location: Just south of Wassamu Pass
    Why visit: Famous backcountry ski touring peak (547m)
    Description: Perfect triangular pyramid with south, northwest, and northeast slopes. Known for "cold powder snow that Horokanai is famous for."
    For us? ‚ùå Not equipped (no touring gear, avalanche equipment)
    Worth knowing: Shows the quality of snow in this region!

    üçú Wassamu Town Center

    Distance: Walking distance from ski area
    What's there: Small rural town, convenience stores, local restaurants
    Vibe: Authentic Hokkaido farm town (famous for pumpkins and cabbage!)
    Quick stop: Grab onigiri or snacks for the road

    üöÇ Wassamu Station (JR Soya Main Line)

    Location: Visible from Route 40
    Why mention it: Historic station on the line to Wakkanai (northernmost city in Japan)
    Photo op: Classic Hokkaido rural station if you're into rail culture

    üõÅ Nayoro Onsen Sunpillar (Our Destination!)

    Group-Specific Notes

    Snowboarders

    • ‚úÖ IDEAL for boards ‚Äî Trees well-spaced, no tight squeeze
    • ‚úÖ No flats in tree zones (good pitch, natural fall line)
    • ‚úÖ Pair lift runs at decent speed (not painfully slow)
    • ‚úÖ No rope tows or T-bars (all chairlifts)

    Powder Day Strategy

    • Sleep in! (Opens 10am, powder waits until 2pm)
    • Spend 3-4 hours here (10am-2pm)
    • Drive to Nayoro (2pm-3pm)
    • Arrive at onsen for check-in, soak, dinner

    Why This Day Works

    It's a palate cleanser: - After Kamui (big) and Pippu (bigger), this is intimate and local - Zero pressure, zero crowds, zero competition - Everyone gets fresh tracks all day - Cheap as chips (¬•2,000!) - Short drive to Nayoro onsen (not a marathon travel day)

    Alternative Plan (If Conditions Are Bad)

    • Wassamu is low elevation ‚Äî if forecast is warm or rainy, pivot to:
    • Extra day at Kamui Ski Links (bigger, higher, more options)
    • Drive straight to Nayoro (arrive early, explore town, onsen time)

    Night Skiing Option

    • If we're having TOO much fun and don't want to leave:
    • Night skiing ¬•500 (5pm-8pm)
    • Pair lift has lights
    • Drive to Nayoro after (arrive ~9pm, late onsen soak)
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Might miss included dinner at Sunpillar (confirm timing)
