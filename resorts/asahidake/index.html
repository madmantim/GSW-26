    ASAHIDAKE

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa

    Quick Stats

    • Size: TINY (1 ropeway, 4 groomed cat tracks)
    • Actual terrain: Massive unpatrolled backcountry zone
    • Annual snowfall: ~14m (estimated ‚Äî no one really measures it!)
    • Vertical: 500m (ropeway accessed)
    • Top elevation: 1,600m (highest lift-access in Hokkaido)
    • Season: Mid-December to early May
    • Trail Map: View Map
    • Best for: Advanced/expert powder hunters who love tree skiing and backcountry
    • NOT for: Beginners, intermediates, or anyone who won't ride off-piste

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa
    Distance: ~70km
    Travel time: ~70 minutes
    Route: Asahikawa ‚Üí Route 39 ‚Üí Route 213 (Asahidake Onsen)
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Large parking lot at ropeway base - ¬•500/day for cars (8 bus spaces, 50 car spaces) - Free for ropeway users (check current policy)

    Route notes: - Straightforward highway driving through Daisetsuzan region - Beautiful scenic route through national park - Road is well-maintained but expect winter conditions - Asahidake Onsen village is at top of road (small cluster of hotels + ropeway)

    Terrain & Access Rules

    ‚ö†Ô∏è THIS IS NOT A SKI RESORT ‚Äî IT'S BACKCOUNTRY

    Critical: Asahidake is unpatrolled, uncontrolled backcountry with ropeway access
    - No ski patrol - No avalanche control - No marked runs (4 groomed cat tracks are just exit routes) - Trail maps have warnings: rescue off-piste at your own cost - MUST carry beacon, shovel, probe

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED ‚Äî but you're on your own
    - Essentially the entire mountain is off-piste - Treat as backcountry: assess snow stability, plan routes, manage risks - Active volcano = heavy snow loads on warm ground = increased ava risk - Fumaroles (volcanic vents) in alpine zone can be hazardous

    In-Bounds Trees

    üå≤ Beautiful tree skiing
    - Well-spaced trees in lower 2/3 of ropeway zone - Pick any line you fancy once into the trees - Some zones require route-finding (creek crossings, terrain traps) - Snowboarder-friendly spacing BUT...

    ‚ö†Ô∏è SNOWBOARDER CHALLENGES

    • Bottom third gets flat ‚Äî exit via groomed cat tracks (can be tedious)
    • Many sidecountry lines don't run fall-line ‚Üí shuffling/poling required
    • Creek crossing 2/3 down forces you back to groomed trails
    • Serious flat zones if you pick wrong line
    • Consider hiring a guide if you want to maximize terrain (minimize flat suffering)

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Powder Strategy

    • Hit early AM ‚Äî freshies disappear fast near ropeway top
    • Fan out wider ‚Äî fresh tracks available at lunch if you're willing to hike a bit
    • Short hikes = big rewards ‚Äî sidecountry has steeper pitches, less tracked
    • Alpine touring ‚Äî endless terrain above ropeway (skin/snowshoe required)

    Best Zones (Ropeway Accessed)

    TOP SECTION (Alpine & Upper Trees) - Reasonably open with short steep pitches - Some natural hits (rocks, small cliffs, mushrooms) - Gets tracked quickly but refills often with ~14m/year snowfall - Skier's left has nice steep bowls (short hike required)

    MID SECTION (Trees) - Well-spaced pines and evergreens - Pick your line ‚Äî all routes lead down - Best powder: Protected from wind, stays light and fluffy - Beautiful tree skiing for advanced riders

    LOWER SECTION - Terrain flattens out - Creek crossing forces exit via groomed trails - Groundhog Day feeling (same exit runs repeatedly)

    Sidecountry (Short Hikes)

    • Hike up/out from ropeway top for steeper pitches
    • Skier's left: Short hikes access nice bowls
    • Skier's right: Various lines but many flatten out or end at river (dead ends)
    • Route-finding essential ‚Äî easy to end up in closed valleys requiring trudging

    Alpine Terrain (Skin/Snowshoe)

    • Massive expanse above ropeway
    • Steep bowls, expert lines
    • Weather dependent: Visibility often poor, snow can be wind-scoured
    • Hazards: Fumaroles (stinky volcanic vents), cracks, poor visibility
    • Recommended: Hire a guide for alpine touring

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Tree skiing ‚Äî lower 2/3 of ropeway zone (the real goods!)
    • Short hike zones ‚Äî sidecountry above ropeway top
    • Powder laps ‚Äî anywhere in trees after fresh snow
    • Alpine bowls ‚Äî if you're touring/have a guide

    SKIPPABLE

    • Groomed cat tracks ‚Äî boring! (Only use for exit)
    • Flat zones ‚Äî unavoidable on exit, but not the point

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • Groomed trails ‚Äî mellow cat tracks for when you're tired
    • This is NOT a recovery mountain ‚Äî go elsewhere for cruising

    On-Mountain Food

    LUNCH (Ropeway Building Only)

    Cafeteria (Ropeway Base Building) - Inexpensive, basic ski cafeteria food - Noodles, curry rice, typical Japanese ski food - Nothing fancy but gets the job done - Pro tip: Pork buns from kiosk while waiting for ropeway!

    Food Kiosk - Snacks, drinks, pork buns - Limited selection

    THAT'S IT. No other on-mountain dining options.

    Alternative: Asahidake Onsen Hotels

    • Several hotels in tiny village below ropeway
    • Can visit hotel restaurants for lunch (walk down from ropeway)
    • Better food but costs time/skiing

    Lift Pass Info

    PRICES (2025-26 Season ‚Äî Regular Season)

    Feb 5 = Regular Season (Nov 1 - May 31)

    Pass Type Adult (Jr High+) Child (Elementary)
    Round-trip ¬•2,800 ¬•1,750
    One-way ¬•1,700 ¬•1,100

    KEY NOTES

    • Not a "lift pass" ‚Äî it's a ropeway fare (like a cable car)
    • Ropeway runs every 20 minutes (every 10min on busy days)
    • Limited capacity = natural crowd control
    • Children under elementary age = FREE (one per adult)
    • No multi-day passes available
    • Group discounts available (15+ people): ¬•2,500 adult round-trip

    ROPEWAY OPERATION

    Feb 5 hours: 9:00am - 3:40pm (last down)
    - Hours vary by season (check daily status) - Winter: typically 9am-4pm - Closed mid-November to early December (maintenance) - Frequent wind closures ‚Äî check morning status before driving

    Website: https://asahidake.hokkaido.jp/en/

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚ö†Ô∏è WEATHER-DEPENDENT OPERATION

    BIGGEST ISSUE: Ropeway closes frequently due to high winds
    - Even moderate winds can shut it down - Check operation status morning-of (website has current conditions) - Backup plan essential ‚Äî consider Kurodake (other side of mountain) or bail to another resort

    ‚ùÑÔ∏è WEATHER CAN BE BRUTAL

    • Often poor visibility during peak winter
    • Temperatures can hit -27¬∞C (we're not kidding!)
    • Heavy snowfall = whiteout conditions
    • Wind-scoured snow in alpine zone
    • BUT: This is when the powder is best!

    üêå LOW VERTICAL OUTPUT

    • Only 500m per lap
    • Ropeway every 20min = ~10 runs/day max (without skinning)
    • Relatively leisurely day compared to lift-serviced resorts
    • This is backcountry pace ‚Äî quality over quantity

    üéø WALK TO SKI

    • Ropeway built for hikers, not skiers
    • Must walk uphill from top station before you can drop in
    • Annoying but minor

    üèîÔ∏è ACTIVE VOLCANO

    • Fumaroles emit steam and sulfurous gas (stinky!)
    • Some cracks/hazards in alpine zone
    • Generally safe but adds to backcountry hazards

    üö´ NO AMENITIES

    • No rental shop (bring your own gear or rent in Asahikawa)
    • No terrain park, no pipe
    • Tiny village (Asahidake Onsen) = very limited nightlife/restaurants
    • National park = minimal development

    üèÇ SNOWBOARDERS: MANAGE EXPECTATIONS

    • Trees are great
    • Flat exits are tedious
    • Sidecountry has flat traps
    • Strong recommendation: Hire a guide to maximize good terrain and avoid flat suffering

    Group-Specific Notes

    üèÇ Snowboarders: Real Talk

    This mountain will test your patience on the flats, but reward you in the trees.

    Pros: - Tree spacing is perfect for boards - Powder is world-class (dry, deep, cold) - Minimal crowds = fresh tracks - Natural hits everywhere

    Cons: - Flat exit runs are tedious (will test your shuffle game) - Sidecountry has flat traps if you pick wrong line - Only 500m vertical per lap

    Recommendation: - Hire a guide for first runs ‚Äî they'll show you lines that minimize flats - Bring poles for shuffling - Manage expectations: this is backcountry, not a resort - If you hate flats, consider skipping and skiing Kamui instead

    üéø Advanced/Expert Skiers

    You will love this place.
    - Endless terrain if you're willing to skin - Tree skiing is sublime - Powder quality is as good as it gets - Hire a guide to unlock alpine zones

    üéí Gear Checklist

    MUST BRING: - Beacon, shovel, probe (non-negotiable) - Fat powder skis/board - Warm layers (temps can hit -27¬∞C) - Goggles (visibility often poor) - Poles for snowboarders (seriously!) - Water/snacks (limited on-mountain options)

    Optional but recommended: - Splitboard or touring setup (if you want alpine zones) - Face mask/balaclava (it's COLD) - Extra batteries (cold kills them fast)
