    PIPPU SKI AREA

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Small (9 runs, 3 lifts + rope tow)
    • Suitable for: 1 day (perfect recovery resort)
    • Annual snowfall: Lower than major Hokkaido resorts (low elevation)
    • Vertical: 330m
    • Elevation: 250-580m
    • Best for: Recovery skiing, off-piste practice, empty slopes, dirt-cheap skiing
    • Trail Map: View Map

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa
    Distance: ~26km
    Travel time: ~35-40 minutes
    Route: Asahikawa ‚Üí Route 39 northeast ‚Üí Pippu
    Exit: Hokkaido Hifukita IC (3 min from expressway exit)
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at resort base - Ample space (this place is never crowded)

    Route notes: - Straightforward drive on Route 39 - Very close to expressway exit - Well-signed in Japanese („Å¥„Å£„Å∑„Çπ„Ç≠„ÉºÂ†¥)

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED ‚Äî This is huge for a recovery day!
    - No pass-pulling risk - Tree skiing permitted (on skier's left side) - Ski patrol used to gaijin in the trees - Perfect for learner powder hounds ("powder puppies")

    Tree Skiing Zones

    • Skier's left of resort ‚Äî main tree skiing area
    • Mellow pitch
    • Moderately spaced trees (not too tight)
    • Small sections (great for experimenting)
    • Easy to navigate back to lifts

    Sidecountry

    • Very accessible ‚Äî obvious routes, no Travis Rice skills required
    • Follow summer road for 5-15 min walk/skin
    • Various tree spacing and moderate pitch
    • Egress: 5 min skin or walk along road back to resort
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è We probably won't push it ‚Äî this is a recovery day!

    In-Bounds Advanced Runs

    • Deep Snow Course (Course #9) ‚Äî 1,300m, up to 36¬∞ pitch, often has actual deep snow
    • Golden Course (Course #8) ‚Äî 1,200m, 30¬∞ max, wild moguls and steep wall

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Why Pippu is Perfect for Tired Legs

    • Usually empty ‚Äî often feels like a private resort
    • Short, manageable laps ‚Äî not exhausting
    • Mellow off-piste ‚Äî no commitment required
    • Super cheap ‚Äî won't feel guilty leaving early
    • Onsen next door ‚Äî soak those legs!

    Best Tree Zones (Snowboarder-Friendly)

    • Skier's left trees (off Deep Snow Course / Golden Course)
    • Well-spaced pines
    • Easy entry/exit
    • Perfect practice zone for tree riding
    • Small zones = low commitment

    Powder Strategy

    • ‚úÖ Fresh tracks last LONGER here (low crowds)
    • Hit trees in morning if there's fresh snow
    • School groups usually stick to Family/Strawberry courses (right side)
    • Defense force training groups = hilarious to watch (old white skis!)

    Terrain Notes

    • On-piste: 30% beginner, 50% intermediate, 20% advanced
    • Real goods: Off-piste trees + Deep Snow Course
    • Longest run: Strawberry Course (2,200m) ‚Äî mellow cruiser
    • Best for recovery: Family Course (2,000m, 13¬∞ average) or Diamond Course

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Deep Snow Course ‚Äî if legs permit, this is the most fun on-piste
    • Skier's left trees ‚Äî perfect for mellow powder practice
    • Strawberry Course ‚Äî longest run (2,200m), great warm-up

    PERFECT FOR RECOVERY

    • Family Course ‚Äî 2,000m, 13¬∞ average, super mellow
    • Diamond Course ‚Äî 1,500m, 16¬∞ average, "for people who just can't stop skiing"
    • Silver Course ‚Äî 700m, smooth intermediate cruiser

    SKIPPABLE

    • Beginner Course ‚Äî very short (400m), 8¬∞ ‚Äî only for absolute beginners
    • Kappa Course ‚Äî 600m, basically a bunny hill ("mini skiers' home ground")

    LAPS FOR THE KEEN

    If someone (Piers?) wants to charge despite it being recovery day: - Repeat Deep Snow Course + Golden Course - Venture into skier's left trees - Explore sidecountry (5-15 min walk out)

    ‚õ∑Ô∏è SMALL RESORT = LIMITED TERRAIN

    üå°Ô∏è LOW ELEVATION = VARIABLE SNOW

    • SW facing aspect (sun can affect snow)

    • Fresh snow is best (tracks out slower here anyway)

    üèóÔ∏è AGING INFRASTRUCTURE

    • What remains is well-maintained but basic
