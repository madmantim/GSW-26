    HOSHINO TOMAMU

    Base: Tomamu The Tower (ON-SITE! Ski-in/ski-out)

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Medium (29 runs, 6 lifts)
    • Suitable for: 2-3 day stay, excellent sidecountry destination
    • Annual snowfall: ~8-9m (underreported as 4.8m by resort)
    • Vertical: 585m
    • Top elevation: 1,171m
    • Base elevation: 586m
    • Trail Map: Download PDF
    • Longest run: 4,200m (Silver Bell ‚Üí Beginner's Choice)
    • Best for: Tree skiing, sidecountry/backcountry access, upmarket family experience
    • Unique attractions: Ice Village (nightly winter wonderland), Terrace of Frosted Tree

    From Our Accommodation

    WE'RE STAYING ON-SITE! ‚≠ê

    Base: Tomamu The Tower ‚Äî True ski-in/ski-out accommodation
    Distance: 0km (literally at the base of Tower Mountain)
    Parking: Resort parking included

    Key advantages: - Roll out of bed and onto the lifts - Short lunch breaks possible (run back to room) - Multiple on-mountain + in-hotel dining options - Access to Ice Village in the evening - Warm up/gear storage super convenient

    Getting here from Sapporo (Feb 1): - Distance: ~150km from Sapporo - Travel time: ~2 hours via Expressway - Route: Sapporo ‚Üí Chitose ‚Üí Route 274 ‚Üí Tomamu - Google Maps: View route

    En route options: - Mount Racey ski resort (morning session before driving to Tomamu) - Chitose area restaurants

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED ‚Äî with registration required

    Official designated off-piste zones: - Multiple marked off-piste areas on both Tower Mountain and Tomamu Mountain - Registration required: Must register at Resort Center before accessing - GPS tracking app: Download required app (allows ski patrol to track location) - Season: Off-piste areas typically open from mid-January onwards - Rating: Resort rates all off-piste as "expert," though realistically they're more advanced (not super steep, no cliffs/cornices)

    Off-piste terrain characteristics: - Well-gladed trees (good spacing for snowboards) - Some bamboo obstacles (fun when snow is deep, annoying when it's shallow) - Wedge-shaped zones on Tomamu Mountain side (try to steer you away from creek) - Wide former piste areas on Tower Mountain side (old lift line access via short hike or traverse)

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Early season note: Resort doesn't get huge early snow volumes like coastal resorts, so expect bamboo navigation in December/early January

    Sidecountry/Backcountry

    ‚úÖ PERMITTED ‚Äî with proper registration

    This is a MAJOR highlight of Tomamu!

    Top backcountry gate (past Cloud Walk): - PRISTINE POWDER ‚Äî north-facing aspect (not sun-affected like in-bounds) - ~300m vertical for sidecountry access - Wide open trees where you can fly - Some bushy zones, but navigable - Snowboarders: Extensive traversing required (may get less vertical) - Ski tourers: Can continue to valley floor for full descent (bliss!)

    Frontside backcountry gate (via Cloud Walk): - Varied terrain when fresh - Good ski touring/splitboard access to adjacent mountain - Multiple bowls open up with touring setup - Can dabble in trees and pop back in-bounds - Warning: Lower sections are REALLY FLAT (slog for boarders, bring touring gear if possible) - Former ski area below for road/train pickup if you commit

    Requirements: - Submit climbing plans at main lodge - Proper avalanche awareness (though Tomamu is not super avalanche-prone) - ‚ö†Ô∏è Not for us if we don't have touring/ava gear ‚Äî but worth considering hiring guide

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Best Strategy

    üèîÔ∏è The real goods at Tomamu are OUTSIDE the ropes

    Powder game plan: - Early AM: Hit sidecountry gates FIRST (top gate = best powder, north-facing aspect) - Mid-morning: Work the designated off-piste zones (trees on Tomamu Mountain side) - Afternoon: If sunny, avoid Tower Mountain front side (south-facing, gets baked) - Powder days: Tower Mountain trees are wide and fun, but quality drops faster in sun

    Snowboarder-Specific Beta

    • ‚úÖ Tree spacing is generally good (well-gladed)
    • ‚úÖ In-bounds off-piste zones are board-friendly
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Sidecountry requires traversing ‚Äî may need to hike/pole certain sections
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Backcountry commitment means FLAT runouts (consider touring gear or be prepared to hike out)
    • ‚úÖ Romance Chair is hilariously slow (brings new meaning to "chair time")

    Weather & Wind Notes

    • Gondola: Old and poorly maintained, prone to wind holds (especially during southerly storms)
    • When gondola closes: Tomamu Express quad gets slammed with queues
    • Snow from southerlies: Tomamu gets decent accumulation from southerly systems (better than Furano)
    • When it swings westerly: Game on at Tomamu (wind calms, lifts reopen)

    Tree Zones (In-Bounds)

    Tower Mountain side: - Wide area including former lift-serviced piste - Can hike up old lift line or traverse in for fewer turns - ‚ö†Ô∏è Aspect: South-facing = sun-baked after clear days (prioritize morning or cloudy days)

    Tomamu Mountain side: - Wedge-shaped zones (keep away from creek) - More consistent quality - Better tree density

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Top backcountry gate (north-facing sidecountry) ‚Äî THE highlight, pristine powder
    • Off-piste tree zones ‚Äî register and explore all designated areas
    • Cloud Walk backcountry access ‚Äî for adventurers with proper gear
    • Terrace of Frosted Tree ‚Äî spectacular hoarfrost views at 1,088m (KUMO Cafe at top)

    SKIP IF TIGHT ON TIME

    • Black runs (No Gravity, others) ‚Äî only 4 blacks, max 35¬∞, not that steep, often bumped up
    • Tower Mountain groomers on sunny days ‚Äî south-facing, gets crusty
    • Romance Chair ‚Äî slowest lift in Japan (use only as connector if necessary)

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • Silver Bell ‚Üí Beginner's Choice ‚Äî 4,200m, gentle cruiser, longest run in resort
    • Gondola descent trail ‚Äî winding mellow blue from top to base (watch for snow scooters!)
    • Nipo Chair area ‚Äî super gentle, in front of The Tower

    FOR NON-SKIERS / EASY DAYS

    • Terrace of Frosted Tree ‚Äî gondola up, amazing views, KUMO Cafe
    • Ice Village ‚Äî evening wonderland (open ~Dec 10 - March 14)
    • Mina-Mina Beach ‚Äî 80m x 30m indoor wave pool, 30¬∞C+ temp (summer in winter!)

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚ö†Ô∏è GONDOLA WIND HOLDS

    MAJOR ISSUE ‚Äî Old gondola is poorly maintained, goes on wind hold frequently - Happens especially during southerly storms - When gondola closes, Tomamu Express quad gets SLAMMED with queues - Backup plan: Focus on Tower Mountain lifts (if wind isn't too severe there too) - Check lift status: Tomamu Dashboard

    ‚òÄÔ∏è ASPECT ISSUES

    • Tower Mountain: Mostly south-facing (sunny = crusty after clear days)
    • Tomamu Mountain: Southeast aspect (slightly better)
    • Solution: Hit Tower Mountain trees early AM, or prioritize sidecountry north-facing zones

    üí∞ UPSCALE PRICING

    • Resort caters to high-end market (families, Club Med, Asian tourists)
    • Everything costs more than typical Japanese resort
    • Budget accordingly

    üè¢ "RESORT" VIBE (NOT A VILLAGE)

    • No traditional Japanese village or town
    • Very curated, purpose-built during Japan's bubble era (~$1 billion investment)
    • Towering hotels look somewhat out of place
    • Limited nightlife (resort bars/restaurants only)
    • Zero local izakaya culture
    • Pro: Modern facilities, English-speaking staff
    • Con: Lacks authentic Japanese ski town charm

    üéå WESTERN CROWD

    • Fewer western tourists than Niseko (mostly Asian guests, especially families)
    • Club Med brings international crowd but keeps to themselves
    • English available but you'll stand out more

    ‚ùÑÔ∏è EARLY SEASON SNOW

    • Not renowned for huge early December volumes (unlike coastal resorts)
    • Our trip is Feb 2 = PRIME SEASON ‚úÖ
    • Snowmaking on main trails supplements natural coverage

    üéø CABIN FEVER RISK

    • If staying multiple days, the "resort bubble" can feel isolating
    • Solution: We're only here 2 nights (Feb 1-2), plus we'll be skiing all day!
    • Ice Village provides evening entertainment
