    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚ùÑÔ∏è EXTREME COLD

    BIGGEST FACTOR: Prepare for -20¬∞C to -30¬∞C
    - Coldest resort on our entire trip (latitude 44¬∞N, deep inland) - "Silky snow" only exists because of these temps - Diamond dust and sun pillar phenomena at these temps - Bring: face masks, neck warmers, hand/toe warmers, layering - Plan warming breaks every 60-90 minutes

    üö´ STRICT ROPE POLICY

    • Patrol is known to be hostile to boundary violations
    • "Roped to within an inch of its life" (TGR forums)
    • Plenty of in-bounds terrain ‚Äî no need to duck ropes
    • Focus on 4 designated tree areas instead

    ‚è∞ EARLY TREE RUN CLOSURES

    • Tree areas close at 3:00pm (main lifts run until 4:30pm)
    • Plan tree laps for morning/early afternoon
    • Don't wait until late day for the 1,400m run

    üó£Ô∏è MINIMAL ENGLISH

    • "Barely any English spoken" (Powderhounds)
    • Trail map and lunch menus have some English
    • Use Google Translate for restaurant orders
    • Staff are friendly despite language barrier
    • This is authentic, local Japan ‚Äî embrace it!

    üèîÔ∏è SMALL RESORT SCALE

    • 3 lifts, 12-13 runs ‚Äî this is NOT a multi-day destination
    • Perfect for 1-2 days (we have 1 day here, 1 day at Horotachi)
    • Advanced skiers may find on-piste limiting
    • Tree runs are the main attraction for our crew

    üå´Ô∏è VISIBILITY

    • Extreme cold can create ice fog/diamond dust
    • Beautiful but can limit visibility
    • Have a flexible plan if conditions are flat light
