    FURANO SKI RESORT

    Base: Tomamu The Tower (Hoshino Resorts)

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Large by Japanese standards (28 runs, 9 lifts + 2 gondolas)
    • Suitable for: 2-3 day visit (but great base for multi-area trips)
    • Annual snowfall: ~9m (less than coastal resorts, but drier/lighter)
    • Vertical: 839m (964m claimed but official is 839m)
    • Top elevation: 1,074m
    • Base elevation: 235m
    • Trail Map: Download PDF
    • Best for: Long groomed runs, tree skiing, sidecountry powder
    • Season: Late November to early May (Nov 29, 2025 - May 6, 2026)
    • Ikon Pass: Included from 2025-26 season (7 days on full pass)

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Tomamu The Tower (Hoshino Resorts, Tomamu)
    Distance: ~60km (39-41 miles)
    Travel time: ~55 minutes to 1 hour
    Route: Tomamu ‚Üí Route 38 south ‚Üí Route 237 northwest to Furano
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at resort base (both Furano Zone and Kitanomine Zone) - Multiple lots near ropeway/gondola stations - Well-marked, easy access

    Route notes: - Straightforward highway driving - Route 237 is scenic through central Hokkaido farmland - Morning session plan: depart early, ski ~4 hours, drive to Asahikawa - Blue Pond detour: Add 40min if visiting Biei's famous Blue Pond (between Furano and Asahikawa)

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED (with discretion) ‚Äî Huge upgrade!
    - Official ban no longer enforced (as of recent years) - Be discreet ‚Äî don't duck ropes in front of patrol - Extensive sidecountry between Furano Zone and Kitanomine Zone - No pass-pulling if you're sensible about it

    Backcountry Access

    • Gates available at top of Furano Zone
    • Requires proper avalanche gear (beacon, shovel, probe)
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Not for us (no ava equipment/training planned)
    • Guided tours available to nearby Tokachidake backcountry

    In-Bounds Trees & Sidecountry

    • Link Chair area = BEST ZONE (between two resort zones)
    • Skier's right (Furano Zone): Lines drop into drainage, return via goat-track
    • Skier's left: Sheltered gullies (but some exit away from resort ‚Äî tricky)
    • Well-spaced trees, snowboarder-friendly
    • Sheltered aspect = powder holds up well after storms

    Premium Zone

    • Steepest run (34¬∞ max, 23¬∞ average, 830m long)
    • Off-piste area, left ungroomed
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Not always open (depends on snowfall/ava conditions)
    • Limited avalanche control

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Best Sidecountry Zones (Snowboarder-Friendly)

    • Link Chair Area (PRIORITY #1)

      • Between Furano Zone and Kitanomine Zone
      • Accessible from top of Link Chair OR Furano Gondola
      • Multiple gullies funnel back to lifts (both zones)
      • Best feature: Lifts run in low wind (when other resorts shut down)
      • Can lap this zone all day on storm days
      • Short hikes above chair for bonus terrain (but ava-prone ‚Äî be careful)

    • Skier's Right (Furano Zone)

      • Various sustained lines dropping into drainage
      • Return via goat-track along drainage (passes large concrete dam)
      • Longer hike out but quality powder
      • Less tracked than Link Chair area

    • Premium Zone (if open)

      • Steepest terrain, left ungroomed
      • Powder paradise after fresh snow
      • Check daily status (often closed)

    Powder Strategy

    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Growing popularity (Ikon Pass + international visitors)
    • Hit sidecountry early AM ‚Äî crowds stick to groomers first
    • Link Chair opens 9am (Furano Ropeway 8:30am)
    • Entries/exits can be obscure ‚Äî takes time to track out
    • Storm day advantage: Furano's sheltered east aspect keeps lifts running when coastal resorts close (wind-hold)

    Terrain Notes

    • On-piste: Long, fast groomed runs (former FIS World Cup courses)
    • Real gems: Sidecountry trees between zones
    • East-facing = rain shadow of Mt Furano-Nishi (less snow, more sun)
    • Better weather/visibility than coastal resorts like Niseko
    • Ungroomed runs left natural for powder

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Link Chair sidecountry ‚Äî prime zone, accessible in all conditions
    • Premium Zone ‚Äî if open, this is the steepest/best
    • Long groomed cruisers ‚Äî A2, A3, F1 (1.7-1.9km long)
    • Skier's right drainage lines ‚Äî for committed powder hunters

    SKIPPABLE

    • Beginner Zone ‚Äî free area with snow escalators (families only)
    • Lower Kitanomine ‚Äî mellow beginner terrain (good recovery runs though)

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • F1 (Kitanomine) ‚Äî 1,900m gentle cruise
    • A2 (Furano) ‚Äî 1,750m intermediate, well-groomed
    • G1 (Kitanomine) ‚Äî 1,080m beginner-friendly

    On-Mountain Food

    ‚ö†Ô∏è SEVERELY LIMITED ‚Äî Powderhounds specifically warns about this!

    Furano Zone (Ropeway Base)

    • Ramen Restaurant "Hokkaido Tomikawa" (Ropeway Base Station)

      • ‚≠ê Best bet ‚Äî branch of famous Furano ramen shop
      • Wide variety of ramen
      • Table and counter seats
      • Quick service

    • Restaurant Downhill (Near Ropeway Station)

      • Casual log house-style
      • Standard ski cafeteria fare

    Kitanomine Zone (Gondola Terminal)

    • "Ezo no Ajidokoro Cise" (2F Gondola Terminal)

      • Local Furano gourmet
      • Regional specialties

    • "Furano Curry Jam" (1F Gondola Terminal)

      • Branch of famous Furano curry restaurant
      • Wide variety of curry dishes

    STRATEGY

    • Limited options = pack snacks (onigiri from conbini)
    • Morning session plan works perfectly (eat lunch in Asahikawa)
    • Hotel breakfasts at Tomamu before departing

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚òÄÔ∏è LOWER SNOWFALL (Pro/Con)

    Con: 9m average vs 15m at coastal resorts (Niseko, Rusutsu)
    Pro: Drier, lighter powder + more bluebird days
    Con: Bad seasons are ROUGH (7-8m = lean pickings)
    Pro: East-facing sheltered aspect = great on storm days

    Bottom line: Furano excels when others are closed (wind), but can lag in dry spells.

    üå¨Ô∏è STORM DAY HERO

    • Sheltered east aspect = lifts run in high wind
    • When Niseko/Rusutsu are on wind-hold, Furano is lapping powder
    • Link Chair + Furano Gondola = low-wind-affected access

    üìà GROWING CROWDS

    • Ikon Pass = more international visitors
    • Powder gets tracked faster than it used to
    • Still MUCH less crowded than Niseko
    • Lift queues sometimes problematic (weekends/holidays)

    üçú LIMITED ON-MOUNTAIN FOOD

    • "Severely lacking" per Powderhounds review
    • Only 4 restaurants for entire resort
    • Pack snacks or plan lunch in town

    üèîÔ∏è BASE FOR MULTI-AREA TRIPS

    • Great hub for day trips: Asahidake, Kamui, Tomamu, Sahoro, Tokachidake
    • Not ideal if locked into pre-paid packages (we have cars = perfect)

    üè® AVOID BIG HOTELS (Not Our Problem)

    • Shin Furano Prince Hotel / Furano Prince Hotel = soulless, expensive, off to the side
    • Shuttle buses required, captive dining
    • We're not staying there ‚Äî just FYI for future reference

    Nearby Attractions (On Travel Path)

    üõÅ Onsen (Tokachidake Area ‚Äî ~40min from Furano)

    • Fukiage Roten-no-yu üåü

      • Distance: 40min drive from Furano (Mt Tokachidake)
      • Why visit: FREE, 24-hour, outdoor-only, in the woods
      • Type: Mixed gender (bring bathing suit if shy)
      • Vibe: Wild onsen under the stars, no artificial lighting
      • Time needed: 30-60 minutes
      • Perfect for: After-ski dip before driving to Asahikawa

    • Hakuginsou Hot Spring Centre

      • Distance: 40min from Furano (Fukiage Onsen area)
      • Why visit: 12 baths (male/female/mixed outdoor areas)
      • Features: Sauna, family-friendly mixed zones
      • Time needed: 1-2 hours

    • Ryounkaku Onsen

      • Distance: 40min from Furano
      • Why visit: 1,280m elevation, clifftop location, panoramic Tokachi volcano views
      • Features: Red-brown water, outdoor + indoor baths, sauna
      • Vibe: Dramatic high-altitude onsen experience
      • Time needed: 1-2 hours

    üç∫ Hop Kotan Brewing (Kamifurano)

    Distance: ~20min from Furano Ski Resort (in Kamifurano town)
    Why visit: Hokkaido craft brewery using local hops
    Beers: ORIGINALS series (100% Kamifurano hops), FREEDOMS (seasonal), Harvest Brews
    Taproom: Mountain lodge-style pub (formerly "Furano Brewery Yama no Doxon")
    Time needed: 1 hour (beer flight + snacks)
    Website: hopkotan.com

    üåä Biei Blue Pond (Shirogane)

    Distance: 40min from Furano, 25min from Asahikawa (ON ROUTE!)
    Why visit: Famous turquoise-blue pond (aluminum-rich water from Mt Tokachi dam)
    Nearby: Shirahige Falls (same area)
    Time needed: 15-20 minutes (quick photo stop)
    Best time: Winter = frozen with snow-covered trees (iconic photo)
    Note: Perfect detour on drive from Furano ‚Üí Asahikawa

    üçú Furano Town Restaurants

    Distance: 3km from ski resort (5-10min drive)
    Options: - Authentic izakayas (local favorite: "The Bridge" cocktail bar) - Furano Delice (famous milk pudding) - Many small pensions and B&Bs with local charm Note: We're driving to Asahikawa for dinner, but good to know for future trips

    Group-Specific Notes

    Snowboarders

    • ‚úÖ Link Chair sidecountry = board-friendly (well-spaced trees)
    • ‚úÖ Long groomed runs = excellent for cruising
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Some connector runs can be flat (stay on main routes)
    • ‚úÖ Premium Zone (if open) = steep powder paradise

    Morning Session Strategy

    • Goal: 4-hour powder hit, then drive to Asahikawa
    • Timeline:
    • 8:30am: First lifts (Furano Ropeway opens 8:30am)
    • 8:30-12:30pm: Lap Link Chair sidecountry + groomers
    • 12:30pm: Depart for Asahikawa
    • Optional: Blue Pond photo stop (+20min)
    • Optional: Fukiage onsen dip (+40min)
    • 2:30-3pm: Arrive Asahikawa, check-in Court Hotel

    Weather Considerations

    • Check Premium Zone status morning-of (often closed)
    • If windy/stormy: PERFECT (Furano excels on storm days)
    • If bluebird: Enjoy those long groomers and tree runs
    • Link Chair + Gondola run in most conditions

    Alternative Plan (If Furano Disappoints)

    • We have cars = can pivot to Asahidake early (1hr drive)
    • Furano's lower snowfall means some seasons are lean
    • Don't get locked in ‚Äî flexibility is key
