    KOKUSETSU HOROTACHI

    Base: Nayoro Onsen Sunpillar

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Tiny (4 runs, 1 lift)
    • Suitable for: Half-day to full-day visit
    • Annual snowfall: ~13m (Horokanai town average)
    • Vertical: 217m (150m - 367m elevation)
    • Top elevation: 367m
    • Best for: Powder gem, local vibe, steep terrain for advanced riders
    • Trail Map: View Map
    • Season: Mid-Dec to late March (sporadic from late Feb onward ‚Äî check before going!)

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Nayoro Onsen Sunpillar (Nayoro)
    Distance: ~76km southwest
    Travel time: ~1hr 20min
    Route: Nayoro ‚Üí Route 275 (south) ‚Üí Horokanai ‚Üí 2km west to ski area
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at base (very close to day lodge and lift) - Small lot but uncrowded - Easy access

    Route notes: - Straight shot south on Route 275 - Well-maintained winter roads - Horokanai town is 2km from ski area - Check operating hours before driving! Resort may close early or operate limited days in late season (Fri-Sun only from late Feb)

    Terrain & Access Rules

    ‚ö†Ô∏è OFF-PISTE POLICY: FORBIDDEN (Strictly Enforced!)

    This is critical for us: - Off-piste skiing NOT ALLOWED between courses or in sidecountry - Staff are known as "off-piste Nazis" (Powderhounds' words, not ours!) - Similar to old-school Furano (pre-2010s) - You WILL be reprimanded if caught in trees - Visible areas (under chairlift, skier's right of chair) = absolute no-go zones

    The Trees Are Excellent... But

    Despite the ban, the tree skiing here is legendary among powder freaks: - Well-spaced trees with good pitch - Deep, cold, dry snow (town averages -13¬∞C in winter) - Gullies and creeks require navigation skills - Some zones have easy ingress/egress, others are technical

    Our approach: - Respect the rules (it's their mountain) - Stick to the two ungroomed courses for powder fun - The "Powder Line" (Horotachi Course) is steep and legitimate - If conditions warrant breaking rules, be invisible and accept full self-responsibility

    Hot Tips: Powder Strategy

    Why Powder Freaks Drive Here

    • Extreme cold = best snow quality

      • Horokanai winter avg: -13¬∞C
      • Dec 31, 2020: -32.6¬∞C recorded
      • Record low: -41.2¬∞C (coldest in Japan)
      • Snow is bone-dry, zero moisture

    • Heavy snowfall

      • ~13m annual average
      • 2015: 120cm fell in ONE DAY
      • 2018: 326cm max depth recorded
      • Special Heavy Snowfall Designated Area

    • Low competition

      • Local town population: 1,500 people
      • Even on powder days, rarely crowded
      • 7-8 people total on spring weekdays (per Mojane report)

    Best Strategy for Feb 11

    Timing: - Hit it early if fresh snow overnight - Low elevation + southeast aspect = sun-affected by afternoon - If warm temps, snow quality can sour quickly

    Runs to prioritize: - Horotachi Course (ungroomed powder line) ‚Äî first! - Shirakaba Course (ungroomed trees) ‚Äî second priority - Karamatsu Course (groomed but steep) ‚Äî warm-up/recovery

    Conditions to expect: - Late season (Feb 11) may be icy in AM, softening by midday - Check operating schedule ‚Äî may only be open Fri-Sun by then - If sun comes out, snow quality deteriorates fast (low elevation, sunny aspect)

    On-Mountain Food

    ‚ö†Ô∏è LATE SEASON OPERATIONS

    üå°Ô∏è LOW ELEVATION + SUNNY ASPECT

    • Southeast facing = sun exposure
    • On warm/sunny days, snow quality deteriorates fast
    • Better choice for brutal weather days when higher resorts (Asahidake, Kurodake) have wind holds
    • Best in cold, snowy, overcast conditions

    • Lift blares music and "don't go off-course" announcements (charmingly retro)

    • Accommodation on-site: Horotachi Sanso (basic lodge)
    • No rentals, no lessons, no childcare
    • Bring everything you need

    üèûÔ∏è Lake Shumarinai (Êú±Èû†ÂÜÖÊπñ)

    Type: Japan's largest artificial lake

    Why visit: Stunning fjord-like scenery, winter activities

    Alternative: Focus on soba and onsen instead (more unique to this area).

    Group-Specific Notes

    Snowboarders

    ‚úÖ Good news: - Horotachi and Shirakaba courses are steep enough for speed - No significant flat sections on main powder lines - Tree spacing is good (where accessible)

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Challenges: - Rinkan course may have flat spots - Off-piste is forbidden (normally where boards shine) - Only 1 lift = limited terrain

    Verdict: Fun for a day, especially if fresh snow. The steep ungroomed runs make it worthwhile.

    Last Ski Day Vibes

    This is our final day on snow! üéø - Small, local, low-key atmosphere = perfect way to wind down - Less intense than Asahidake/Kurodake - Enjoy the powder gem without crowds - Post-ski soba and onsen = cultural sendoff - Drive back to Nayoro for final onsen dinner at hotel

    Crowd Strategy

    • Town population: 1,500 people
    • Even powder days are uncrowded
    • Feb 11 is a Wednesday ‚Äî if open, may have resort to ourselves
    • Low competition for fresh tracks

    Weather Backup

    If Horotachi is closed or conditions are poor: - Option 1: Return to Nayoro Piyashiri (skied Feb 10) - Option 2: Drive to Wassamu (31km east, 30min) - Option 3: Skip skiing, explore Horokanai soba culture + Lake Shumarinai
