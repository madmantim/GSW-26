    KURODAKE

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Tiny (2 groomed runs + off-piste/backcountry paradise)
    • Suitable for: HARDCORE EXPERTS ONLY ‚Äî not for intermediates or casual riders
    • Annual snowfall: ~15m (unofficial ‚Äî they don't even bother measuring)
    • Vertical: 850m (ropeway + chairlift: 670m-1,520m) / 1,314m to summit (1,984m via skinning)
    • Top elevation: 1,984m (Mt. Kurodake summit)
    • Best for: Extreme steep terrain, cliffs, chutes, technical tree skiing, backcountry
    • Trail Map: View Map

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Court Hotel Asahikawa (Central Asahikawa)
    Distance: ~66km
    Travel time: ~90 minutes
    Route: Asahikawa ‚Üí Route 39 east ‚Üí Sounkyo Onsen
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at ropeway base (60 cars) - Rarely crowded ‚Äî empty car park = powder day confirmed!

    Route notes: - Scenic drive through Daisetsuzan National Park approach - Well-maintained Route 39 (main highway) - Sounkyo Gorge is spectacular (100m cliffs on both sides) - ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Check ropeway status before leaving ‚Äî often closed for wind/weather - Status page: Kurodake Ropeway (Japanese) - Call ahead: 01658-5-3031

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ FULLY ALLOWED ‚Äî This is THE place for it!
    - No rope enforcement ‚Äî patrollers won't chase you - Off-piste and backcountry are the entire point of Kurodake - ‚ö†Ô∏è NO AVALANCHE CONTROL ‚Äî you're 100% on your own - Must have proper backcountry gear (beacon, shovel, probe) - Highly recommended: hire a guide (terrain is cliff-riddled and complex)

    Backcountry Access

    • Summit access via skinning (1,520m chairlift top ‚Üí 1,984m summit)
    • Huge backcountry area beyond summit (Daisetsuzan interior)
    • 1-2 day traverse to Asahidake possible for advanced parties
    • ‚ö†Ô∏è NOT FOR US ‚Äî need touring gear, ava knowledge, serious experience

    Terrain Zones

    Zone 1: Under the Ropeway (670m-1,300m) - Cliff-riddled, very steep tree skiing - Technical chutes and couloirs - HARD routefinding ‚Äî cliffs funnel you into specific lines - Gets tracked quickly despite low crowds (limited options) - Some shelter from wind/sun (holds snow quality better) - ‚ö†Ô∏è GUIDE HIGHLY RECOMMENDED ‚Äî wrong turn = cliff/death or brutal hike

    Zone 2: Under the Chairlift (1,300m-1,520m) - 2 groomed runs (beginner-friendly) - Mellow off-piste - ‚ö†Ô∏è VERY FLAT ‚Äî not ideal for snowboards - Good views but not worth the trip alone

    Zone 3: Summit Skin Terrain (1,520m-1,984m) - Moderately pitched sub-alpine terrain - Well-spaced trees, fun angle for powder - More exposed to wind (can get slabby) - Epic views on bluebird days - ‚ö†Ô∏è Backcountry skills required ‚Äî no patrol, no control

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    The Reality Check

    Kurodake is not like other Hokkaido resorts. This is: - The steepest terrain in Hokkaido - Unpredictable and often closed - Requires advanced skills and route-finding - Best visited with a guide unless you're very experienced

    Powder Strategy

    • Morning weather check is CRITICAL ‚Äî ropeway often closed for wind
    • If it's running, GO EARLY ‚Äî tracks fill in fast (despite low crowds)
    • Focus on ropeway terrain for steep trees and technical lines
    • Bring helmet, beacon, shovel, probe ‚Äî non-negotiable
    • GPS/topo map app recommended ‚Äî easy to get lost/cliffed out

    Best Terrain (with guide)

    • Tight, steep trees under ropeway ‚Äî some of the best in Hokkaido (Pete from Powder Project agrees)
    • Technical chutes and couloirs
    • Every line feels different (intricate terrain)
    • Range from "fun-hard" to "hard-hard"

    Snowboarder Notes

    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Chairlift terrain is FLAT ‚Äî not ideal for boards
    • Ropeway terrain is steep enough but very tight trees (need strong technique)
    • If conditions are heavy/crusty, you'll struggle to turn in time to avoid trees
    • If you're not confident in tight, steep trees, skip this resort

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS (if conditions align)

    • Under the ropeway ‚Äî this is why you're here
    • Summit hike on a bluebird day (views are spectacular)
    • Beer Grill Canyon in Sounkyo after (Italian food, war stories)

    SKIPPABLE

    • Chairlift terrain ‚Äî unless you're just sightseeing or skinning up

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • None. This isn't a recovery resort.

    On-Mountain Food

    LUNCH

    Ropeway Top Station Cafeteria (5th Station, 1,300m) - Cute little spot with views (on clear days) - Menu: Ramen, katsu curry, gyoza, pancakes - Basic ski cafeteria fare, but yummy pancakes noted - ‚ö†Ô∏è CASH ONLY ‚Äî no credit cards

    Alternative: Bring your own food - Convenience stores in Sounkyo (Seicomart, 7-Eleven) - Brown-bag it if you're lapping the ropeway

    DINNER (Sounkyo Town)

    Beer Grill Canyon (Canyon Mall, town center) - ‚≠ê Top pick ‚Äî legendary skier/snowboarder hangout - Italian restaurant & bar - Amazing Italian food (wedding-style multi-course gourmet meals) - Well-priced despite quality - ‚ö†Ô∏è Beer is good, wine is questionable (stick to beer!) - Free Wi-Fi - The place to swap powder stories

    Hotel dining: - Most hotels offer half-board with kaiseki dinners - Pensions serve simple Japanese dishes - Limited stand-alone restaurants in Sounkyo

    Gotchas & Quirks

    ‚ö†Ô∏è WEATHER CLOSURES (BIG DEAL)

    THIS IS THE #1 ISSUE: - Ropeway closes frequently for wind, snow, avalanche risk - Sounkyo is "the roof of Hokkaido" ‚Äî exposed, socked in, brutal weather - Even when open, visibility can be zero - Maintenance closures: Jan-Feb for several weeks (check dates!) - 2026 dates: March 2-19 (maintenance) - We're visiting Feb 6 ‚Äî should be open BUT weather-dependent - ‚ö†Ô∏è CHECK STATUS MORNING-OF ‚Äî don't assume it's running - Call: 01658-5-3031 - Website: rinyu.co.jp/kurodake

    üßó NOT FOR BEGINNERS/INTERMEDIATES

    • This is hardcore expert terrain only
    • If you're not willing to earn your turns, don't come
    • If you can't handle tight trees and cliffs, don't come
    • If you need groomed runs, go literally anywhere else

    üí∞ CASH SOCIETY

    • No credit cards at ropeway (tickets, food, rentals)
    • Bring cash! ATMs in Sounkyo: 7-Eleven or post office

    üèîÔ∏è GUIDE RECOMMENDED

    • Terrain is complex, cliff-riddled, avalanche-prone
    • Routefinding under ropeway is hard (even for experienced riders)
    • Visibility often terrible
    • Private guides available from Furano (White Room Tours)
    • Multi-day tours occasionally include Kurodake (flexible itineraries)

    üéø RENTALS = JOKE

    • Geared for tourists/beginners (rear-entry boots from 1980s)
    • No snowboard rentals
    • No backcountry gear rentals
    • Bring your own gear or rent in Asahikawa/Furano before coming

    üè® SOUNKYO TOWN

    • Not a cute onsen village (multi-story concrete hotels)
    • Limited nightlife/restaurants
    • Main activities: skiing, onsen, ice festival (late Jan-March)

    Group-Specific Notes

    Is This Trip Worth It?

    PROS: - If conditions align: some of the best steep tree skiing in Hokkaido - Almost zero crowds (empty car park = powder heaven) - Off-piste paradise (no rope enforcement) - Unique Hokkaido experience (this is NOT typical Japanese skiing) - Sounkyo Gorge scenery is spectacular

    CONS: - High risk of closure (wind, weather, ava conditions) - Requires advanced skills (not suitable for whole group) - Needs guide for safety + finding best lines - 90min drive from Asahikawa (long day trip) - Limited terrain without skinning

    Decision Framework

    GO if: - ‚úÖ Weather is good (check morning-of) - ‚úÖ Ropeway is confirmed running - ‚úÖ Fresh snow in last 24-48hrs (makes the drive worth it) - ‚úÖ We can hire a guide (safety + line knowledge) - ‚úÖ Group is comfortable with expert terrain

    SKIP if: - ‚ùå Ropeway closed or forecast is bad - ‚ùå No fresh snow (tracked steep trees = not fun) - ‚ùå Group wants mellow laps (go to Kamui or Pippu instead) - ‚ùå Can't secure a guide (safety concern)

    Backup Plan Options (Feb 6)

    If Kurodake doesn't pan out:

    • Kamui Ski Links (40min from Asahikawa) ‚Äî underrated gem, good tree zones
    • Asahidake (return visit) ‚Äî ropeway may be more reliable than Kurodake
    • Otokoyama Sake Brewery day ‚Äî tour, tastings, Asahikawa ramen lunch
    • Sounkyo Ice Festival + onsen ‚Äî still worth the drive for culture/relaxation

    Snowboarder Considerations

    • ‚ö†Ô∏è Chairlift terrain is FLAT (not worth it for boards)
    • Ropeway terrain is steep but VERY technical (tight trees)
    • Only attempt if:
    • You're very strong in steep, tight trees
    • Snow is light and dry (heavy = hard to turn in time)
    • You're okay with high consequence terrain
    • If in doubt, sit this one out and go to Kamui instead
