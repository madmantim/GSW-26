    SAPPORO KOKUSAI SKI RESORT

    Base: Hotel WBF Sapporo Chuo

    Quick Stats

    • Size: Small (7 courses, 4 lifts - 1 gondola, 2 quads, 1 double)
    • Suitable for: 1-2 day visit
    • Annual snowfall: ~16m (unofficial, but known to exceed Niseko)
    • Vertical: 470m (630m - 1,100m elevation)
    • Top elevation: 1,100m
    • Best for: Tree skiing, powder stashes, backcountry access, local vibe
    • Trail Map: View Map

    From Our Accommodation

    Base: Hotel WBF Sapporo Chuo (Central Sapporo)
    Distance: ~46km
    Travel time: ~60 minutes
    Route: Sapporo ‚Üí Route 230 (southwest toward Jozankei)
    Google Maps: View route

    Parking:
    - Free parking at resort base - Large parking area near gondola - Can get busy on weekends (locals love this place!)

    Route notes: - Direct route via Route 230 through the mountains - Well-maintained winter highway - Jozankei Onsen is on the way (~19km from resort) ‚Äî perfect for apr√®s-ski stop - Alternative route via Otaru possible but longer

    Terrain & Access Rules

    Off-Piste Policy

    ‚úÖ ALLOWED but officially prohibited ‚Äî This is key for us!
    - Off-piste is technically prohibited BUT not policed - No patrollers chasing you with whistles - Locals ride off-piste all the time - Far less enforcement than most Japanese resorts

    Translation: You're good to go. Just be smart about it.

    Backcountry Access

    ‚úÖ EXCELLENT ACCESS ‚Äî One of the best resort-accessed BC zones in Hokkaido
    - Gondola top station (1,100m) provides easy access to: - Mt. Asari (1,281m) ‚Äî classic tour, ~200m elevation gain - Mt. Shirai ‚Äî longer tour with tree runs - Well-known backcountry touring destination since the 1910s - Intermediate-level tours with spectacular tree runs

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Not for us without a guide (no touring gear/ava equipment planned)
    But good to know the terrain is legendary if we wanted to hire a guide!

    In-Bounds Trees

    ‚úÖ SNOWBOARDER HEAVEN
    - Generously spaced trees throughout the resort - Park-like gladed zones with excellent spacing - Multiple powder stashes in tree zones - "Deep not steep" terrain philosophy - Plenty of pitch if you can read terrain well

    Hot Tips: Powder Stashes & Tree Runs

    Best Tree Zones (Snowboarder-Friendly)

    Based on NinjaPowder, Powderhounds, and Whiteroom guides:

    • In-Bounds Tree Pockets

      • Scattered throughout the resort (skier's left side especially)
      • Generously spaced ‚Äî perfect for boards
      • "Farm skiing" strategy: push out to sides, ski fresh all day
      • Less tracked than groomers (crowds stick to pistes)

    • Gullies & Natural Features

      • Band of R-rated cliffs (avoid unless confident)
      • Multiple gullies to explore
      • Snow protected from wind = consistently good conditions
      • North-facing zones hold powder longest

    • Backcountry Tree Runs (with guide only)

      • Mt. Shirai ‚Üí Mt. Asari route = "park-like gullies"
      • 20-30 min run through ancient trees
      • Enormous old-growth trees
      • Feeds back to resort

    Powder Strategy

    • ‚ö†Ô∏è WEEKEND CROWDS ‚Äî This is a local favorite from Sapporo/Otaru
    • Weekday visit = perfect (we're going Friday, borderline)
    • Hit tree zones early ‚Äî crowds stick to groomers first
    • "Farm skiing" approach: work the sides all day for fresh lines
    • Off-piste areas are quieter than pistes

    Terrain Notes

    • Official terrain: 30% beginner, 50% intermediate, 20% advanced
    • Reality: The goods are off-piste
    • Only 1 black run on trail map (would be blue with grooming)
    • Moguls on black run add spice
    • Maximum gradient: 30 degrees (on powder slopes)
    • "Deep not steep" philosophy = perfect for powder surfing

    Best Runs & Areas

    DON'T MISS

    • Tree pockets skier's left ‚Äî best powder stashes
    • Natural gullies ‚Äî protected snow, consistent quality
    • Off-piste zones ‚Äî officially prohibited, actually amazing
    • Views from gondola top ‚Äî Japanese Sea, Otaru Bay, Ishikari Bay on clear days

    SKIPPABLE

    • Groomed beginner zones ‚Äî we're not here for this
    • Terrain park ‚Äî good for beginners/intermediates but not our focus

    RECOVERY RUNS

    • Wide mellow runs (skier's right) ‚Äî when legs are tired
    • Groomed reds ‚Äî limited but pleasant for warm-up laps

    Key Comparisons

    vs. Kiroro (Tomorrow)

    • Kokusai: Smaller, cheaper, more local vibe, off-piste not policed
    • Kiroro: Bigger, pricier, more international, official off-piste zones
    • Snow: Both epic (Kokusai might even edge it)
    • Crowds: Kokusai busier on weekends, Kiroro has Club Med crowds
    • Verdict: Kokusai for powder hunters, Kiroro for variety

    vs. Niseko

    • Kokusai: Less crowded, cheaper, more Japanese vibe, different weather pattern
    • Niseko: Bigger, more infrastructure, busier, international hub
    • Snow: Kokusai often gets MORE
    • Terrain: Niseko bigger, but Kokusai's trees are excellent
    • Verdict: Kokusai is the local's choice for a reason

    Why This Resort Matters

    From NinjaPowder guides Felix & Pat:

    "Cheaper cousin to Kiroro, less crowd, equally good snow cover and terrain. Less under the limelight so cheaper in general without compromise with quality."

    Perfect for: A powder day without the Niseko/Kiroro price tag or crowds, with tree runs that'll make you grin.
